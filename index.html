Do I Qualify for a Roundup Lawsuit?

You may qualify if you meet the following criteria:

  • You were a regular user of Roundup (or other glyphosate weed killers)
  • You have been diagnosed with non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, leukemia, or another form of cancer linked to Roundup exposure

Qualifying cancers include Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma (B-cell or T-cell types), Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia, Multiple Myeloma, and other lymphomas.

Even if your loved one has already passed away from their cancer, you may still be able to file a claim on their behalf.

How Roundup Exposure Happens

People are exposed to Roundup and its active ingredient glyphosate through various activities.

Home Use

Residential Applications

Homeowners frequently use Roundup for lawn and garden maintenance, applying it to driveways, sidewalks, flower beds, and vegetable gardens.

Products

Various Formulations

Roundup comes in multiple forms including liquid concentrate, ready-to-use sprays, and extended control varieties, all containing potentially harmful chemicals.

Agricultural

Commercial Farming

Agricultural workers face the highest exposure risk, often applying Roundup to large crop areas over many years without proper protective equipment.

Monsanto Knew the Risks

Internal documents revealed during trials show that Monsanto was aware of the potential health risks associated with Roundup but failed to adequately warn consumers.
